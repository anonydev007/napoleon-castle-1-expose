[PRAISE CORNER] The Boundless "Dedication and Responsibility" of Napoleon Castle Property Management

Residents of Napoleon Castle 1

Honestly, I don't know whether to laugh or cry at the security service here. After being "visited" twice and having bicycle parts vanish — totalling over 3 million VND in damages (~$120 USD), I'd like to "commend" how the Property Management works:

A Record-Breaking Memory

The first time my property was stolen, the Management promised to resolve it and then... gently "forgot" for 6 whole months. Perhaps their schedule was so packed that a resident's stolen property simply evaporated from their memory.

World-Class Evasion Skills

The second time I was robbed, I messaged them and waited for one full week with no reply. When I finally went down to confront them in person, I received this earth-shattering excuse:

"There were too many messages, so we haven't had time to reply yet" — Representative of Napoleon Castle 1 Property Management

Apparently, a resident's stolen property isn't "priority" enough for the lobby representatives to spare a moment of their precious golden time.

Bizarre Camera Logic

The perpetrator bent the camera to commit the act. The camera clearly recorded everything, but Management still insisted:

"The face and the act of theft aren't clearly visible, so we bear no responsibility" — Napoleon Castle 1 Property Management

Wait, so just bending the camera makes you automatically innocent? Case closed?

Boundless Kindness (Towards the Perpetrator)

They caught the person who damaged the bicycle, but Management quietly filed a report and then... let them go to "protect the resident's identity" (the perpetrator, that is). As for the victim's damages and rights? Management gave them a report as a souvenir. Done.

The Truth Gets "Steered"

The extracted camera footage shows the perpetrator bending the camera angle, but a few days later Management claimed:

"Nobody bent anything" — Napoleon Castle 1 Management (after reviewing the camera)

Perhaps the camera knew to look away out of... embarrassment? Or maybe cameras at Napoleon have souls?

A Message to Residents

Napoleon residents, take note:

  • Bicycle stolen — deal with it yourself, or contact the authorities on your own
  • Other property — who knows if they'll share the same "deal with it yourself" fate?
Management here only has the responsibility to "contact, connect, and... FILE REPORTS!" So if you plan to buy anything nice, better take it to bed and sleep with it!

The "Silencing" Tactic — Report & Kick from Groups

You'd think Management would focus on resolving residents' losses? Nope. Instead, the Management & HOA chose to... report and kick residents out of the Zalo group and all other communication channels.

Logic: Can't solve the problem? Solve the person raising the problem. Resident complains → kicked from group → no more complaints → "problem solved."

Can't solve the problem? Solve the person raising the problem. — Strategy of Napoleon Castle 1 Management & HOA

It didn't stop there. The Management & HOA also tried to report residents' personal Facebook accounts. They even proactively contacted Facebook group admins to report residents' accounts — all to seal off every avenue of feedback.

Instead of sitting down, having a direct dialogue, and cooperating with residents to find the best solution for everyone — Management & HOA chose to eliminate the voice. The energy spent reporting, kicking, and silencing — if channelled into solving problems, Napoleon Castle 1 would have been a model complex long ago.

If the energy used to report residents was used to solve problems, Napoleon would have been a 5-star complex ages ago.

This is exactly why this website exists — when every channel of dialogue is silenced, residents need a place no one can "kick" them from.

Theft → Complaint → Report → Silence → More theft → ♻️

[PRAISE CORNER - PART 2] The Art of "Being Responsible in a Systematic Way"

Residents of Napoleon Castle 1 · 2026

1. The "Grand" Appearance of the HOA

After days of waiting, the HOA (Homeowners' Association) of Napoleon Castle 1 finally showed up — not to solve problems, but to... demonstrate their presence.

The meeting was organized with full ceremony: tables, chairs, microphones, and professional-grade silence. Residents arrived in full force, carrying thick stacks of complaints. The HOA brought... smiles.

"We've listened, we've noted it down, but what we'll do about it... don't hold your breath!" — The Spirit of Napoleon Castle 1 HOA

2. "Meeting Minutes" — A Remix of the Recording

The highlight of the meeting: the official minutes. But when residents compared the minutes with the actual recording, the results were... impressive.

The minutes stated: "The HOA has received feedback and commits to resolution." The actual recording: the HOA said "This is not within our scope of responsibility."

A masterpiece "remix" — using raw material from reality, but the final product was... far more creative.

"Minutes are minutes. Recordings are recordings. The two don't necessarily have to match." — HOA Logic

3. The "Systematically Responsible" Process

If we had to summarize how the HOA & Property Management of Napoleon Castle 1 operate, here's the standard procedure:

Theft → Complaint → Report → Silence → More theft → ♻️

This loop has been verified by numerous residents, multiple times, over many years. The result? Nobody gets a resolution, but the system runs smoothly.

"Pay management fees to manage your own property" — The Privilege of Napoleon Castle 1 Residents
  • Security cameras: present (but broken when needed)
  • Security guards: present (but invisible)
  • Property Management: present (but deaf)
  • HOA: present (but idle)

4. The Ultimate "Silence" Tactic

After receiving complaints, the HOA/Management deploys the final tactic: absolute silence.

No Zalo replies. No phone answers. No email. No notices. Residents ask 10 times, get answered... 0 times.

"Nobody cares about nobody — Possessions come and go" — The Motto of Napoleon Castle 1 Management

This tactic is so effective that many residents gradually... give up. And that's exactly the goal.

5. A Message to Residents

Don't stay silent. Don't give up. Don't let the HOA & Management continue "being responsible in a systematic way" — which means having no responsibility at all.

Take action:

  • Record every meeting, every call
  • Photograph every report, every notice
